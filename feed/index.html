]]> Cheap” Price

In paying for a service or a product, we all want value, not cheap. Value is getting that maximum benefit for the amount of money we invest in something. Most of the “cheapest wordpress hosting” says it’s cheap, but are you actually getting a good value?

I am not sure if you already know this, but the trick is they show you the price for a 36-month subscription instead of the price  that is to be paid on a monthly basis.

For example, Bluehost – https://www.bluehost.com/products/shared showing that it’s only $3.95/month.

However, when you check out, it will show you it’s actually $3.95/month for 36-month subscription which is at least $142 upfront.

File Count Limit

So most of the hosting providers offer “Unlimited” size storage. However, what you don’t know is they limit the number of files. Yes, you can have as much as storage space you have which would mean you can have the unlimited size but limited number of files.

Here’s the file count limits for the hosting providers:

Digital Ocean – https://www.digitalocean.com/community/questions/is-there-a-limit-on-the-number-of-files-in-a-droplet-node-limitation
Bluehost – https://my.bluehost.com/hosting/help/2274
Godaddy – https://godaddy.com/help/resource-limits-12001
HostGator – https://support.hostgator.com/articles/inode-limits

SSL Certificates

Recently, you have found out about Google penalizing websites that do not have SSL. Hence, you thought of getting one. Some services are offering it for over $100, some are including it in their packages, and there’s even a way to get it for free via Let’s Encrypt

However, in some of the hosting options, they would require you to get a dedicated IP address  per SSL certificate installed.

Number of Websites

Having the “unlimited” disk space offering makes your imagination go wild. But hold it right there, you can only host one website in these “cheap” hosting packages.
